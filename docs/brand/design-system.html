02 — Motion

Easing & timing

Slow-in / slow-out is the #1 amateur-vs-pro tell. Custom curves only — the built-in CSS easings are too weak to feel intentional. Watch the difference:

04 — Rules

Principles every frame obeys

Distilled from 3Blue1Brown, the 12 principles of animation, Gestalt psychology, and information-design research.

01

One idea per moment

Each beat of narration introduces exactly one new thing on screen.

02

Deliberate motion

If you can’t say why it moves, it doesn’t move. Visual and voice say the same thing.

03

One focal point

Size, contrast, and a single focus color make it unambiguous where to look.

04

Progressive disclosure

Build diagrams piece by piece. Never show a finished-but-unexplained frame.

05

Slow in / slow out

Nothing moves at constant velocity except a deliberate scan line.

06

Group with Gestalt

Proximity, similarity, continuity — related things move and sit together.

07

Definition last

Show the picture that makes the idea inevitable, then name it.

08

One bold thing

Spend all boldness on the brace reveal; keep everything else quiet.
